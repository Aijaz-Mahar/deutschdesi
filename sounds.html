Lesson 3: Key Letter Combinations

In German, when certain letters sit together at the start of a word or syllable, their sound changes specifically. Here are the most important ones:

1. The "ST" Rule

When st appears at the start of a word, it sounds like Sht (شٹ).

GermanSoundUrdu/HindiEnglish
StraßeShtrasseسٹراسہ / श्ट्रासेStreet
StadtShtadtشٹاٹ / श्टाटCity
VerstehenVershtay-enفرشٹیہن / फ़रश्टेहनTo Understand
2. The "SP" Rule

When sp appears at the start, it sounds like Shp (شپ).

SpielShpeelشپیل / श्पीलPlay / Game

3. The "CH" Rule

This has two sounds: Soft (sh/h) after i/e, and Hard (Kh - خ) after a/o/u.

This always sounds like a full Sh (ش), exactly like "Sh" in English.
